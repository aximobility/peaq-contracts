% ----------------------------- 3.1 Insurance Risk Oracle
\subsection{Insurance Risk Oracle}

\subsubsection{Problem}
Insurance underwriters in Kenya quote premiums based on patchy fleet data. They charge a "risk surcharge" because they cannot trust the numbers. ROAM (and every fleet operator we talked to) overpays by 15--25\%.

\subsubsection{Solution}
We attest a per-DID risk score on-chain. Append-only, signed by an authorised attestor. Score in $[0, 1000]$. Insurer reads the latest score before quoting. Verifiable. Auditable. Cheap.

% ----------------------------- 3.2 High-Risk Command Vault

\subsection{High-Risk Command Vault}

\subsubsection{Problem}
Fleet-wide actions are risky. "Lock 47 bikes" is one button, and it has to be reversible, auditable, and multi-party. Operator dashboards routinely have a single-sig escape hatch --- that is how fleets get held ransom.

\subsubsection{Solution}
$K$-of-$M$ approval gate. AXI staff propose a high-risk command (lock fleet, mass route override, broadcast more than 100 messages, fund withdrawal). $M$ approvers must sign. Once $K$ reach (out of $M$), the command becomes executable by anyone --- including a regulator if AXI staff disappear.

\subsubsection{Real ROAM scenario}

\begin{scenariobox}[title=Mary's bike pack-temp spike]
KMG~117P shows pack temperature 52$^\circ$C --- risk of fire. AXI ops Brian proposes \texttt{LOCK\_FLEET} on the 3 bikes in the same depot, $K = 2$ of $M = 3$.

\vspace{2pt}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, nosep]
  \item Joy approves \enspace$\cdot$\enspace 1/2 reached
  \item ROAM ops manager approves \enspace$\cdot$\enspace 2/2 reached \enspace$\rightarrow$\enspace status flips to \textbf{Approved}
  \item Anyone calls \texttt{execute(commandId, payload)} \enspace$\rightarrow$\enspace bikes locked
\end{itemize}

The on-chain audit shows: who proposed, who approved, when, and what.
\end{scenariobox}

\subsubsection{Why peaq vs an off-chain Slack approval}
A Slack approval can be repudiated. An on-chain $K$-of-$M$ cannot. The audit trail is the product --- insurance underwriters and regulators want to see operators are practising good governance before they price the premium or licence the fleet.

\subsubsection{Implementation}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{3.8cm} p{11.5cm}}
\toprule
{\bfseries\color{axiprimary}Contract} & \texttt{HighRiskCommandVault.sol} (\textbf{not upgradeable}) \\
\midrule
Threshold range & $1 \le K \le M \le 10$ \\
Expiry window & 1 minute to 30 days \\
Replay-safety & \texttt{commandId} hashes \texttt{(commandType, targetDid, payloadHash, K, M, nowTs, proposer)} \\
Payload binding & \texttt{propose()} stores \texttt{keccak256(payload)}; \texttt{execute()} must present same bytes \\
Permissionless execute & Once $K$ reached, anyone can run it (no value extraction; documented MEV note) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{notebox}[title=Why not upgradeable]
An upgradeable approval gate is a back door. The Vault is permanent --- operators see exactly what they are approving and there is no path for AXI to silently change the rules.
\end{notebox}

% ----------------------------- 3.3 Credential Revocation Registry
\newpage
\subsection{Credential Revocation Registry}

\subsubsection{Problem}
Driver licenses, NTSA inspection certificates, and insurance binders all expire or get revoked. Today fleets find out at the roadside check (about \$300 fine plus a tow-truck bill). Insurers find out at claim time (about \$5{,}000 loss). Verification is paper-based and manual.

\subsubsection{Solution}
Each credential is hashed and registered. When the issuer (NTSA, insurance company, AXI fleet manager) revokes, they call \texttt{revoke(hash, reasonCode)}. Anyone --- insurer, regulator, the driver app itself --- can check \texttt{isRevoked(hash)} for free. Per-issuer scope: only the issuer of a credential can revoke it.

\subsubsection{End-to-end flow}

\begin{center}
\resizebox{\textwidth}{!}{

\subsubsection{Real ROAM scenario}

\begin{scenariobox}[title=ROAM rider Wanjiku's NTSA inspection]
Inspection certificate expires 2026-05-15. NTSA officer revokes:

\hspace{1em}{\ttfamily\color{axiprimary} revoke(hash=0x... (Wanjiku's certificate), reasonCode=2 (expired))}

\vspace{2pt}
At the next checkpoint, the inspector scans the QR code on Wanjiku's bike. The verify app calls \texttt{isRevoked(hash)} $\rightarrow$ \textbf{true}. Wanjiku is pulled aside, not jailed for "fraudulent docs". Saves AXI a court date.
\end{scenariobox}

\subsubsection{Why peaq vs a central NTSA database}
NTSA's portal goes down regularly. Our chain does not. Plus: insurance firms outside Kenya want to sell premium products to AXI fleets without trusting Kenya's central databases --- chain-anchored revocation gives them an independent verification path.
