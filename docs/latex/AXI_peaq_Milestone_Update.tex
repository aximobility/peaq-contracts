\section{Summary}

AXI runs a fleet platform. We monitor vehicles, drivers, batteries, and route data, then turn that into alerts, actions, and reports for the fleet owner.

\subsection{What we built on peaq}

Three smart contracts on Agung testnet:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, nosep]
  \item \textbf{Insurance Risk Oracle} - publishes a per-driver risk score that insurers can read.
  \item \textbf{High-Risk Command Vault} - requires multiple sign-offs before a fleet-wide action runs.
  \item \textbf{Credential Revocation Registry} - lets issuers cancel a license or certificate so anyone can check it instantly.
\end{itemize}

Plus a TypeScript library and command-line tool that talk to peaq's identity, role, and storage features, sign with hardware-backed keys, and post a daily summary of all events to the chain.

\subsection{Where we are today}

\begin{successbox}[title=Status]
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, nosep]
  \item \textbf{51 tests passing} on the local test setup.
  \item \textbf{Agung dry-run deploy successful.} 25 minutes from a blank config to the first signed event.
  \item \textbf{About 80\% of the pre-audit checklist is closed.} Remaining: extra security testing, multi-signature wallet setup, external auditor, and a frozen review tag.
  \item \textbf{Going live on mainnet} needs a 4-of-7 multi-signature wallet and an external audit. Realistic window: 6 to 10 weeks from audit start.
\end{itemize}
\end{successbox}

% ----------------------------- 3.1 Insurance Risk Oracle
\subsection{Insurance Risk Oracle}

\subsubsection{The problem}
Insurers in Kenya price premiums on patchy fleet data, so they add a 15-25\% surcharge they call "uncertainty risk". ROAM and every fleet we spoke to overpays.

\subsubsection{The solution}
We post a risk score (0-1000) for each driver to the chain. Insurers read the latest score before quoting. The log is append-only and signed - they can audit every score themselves.

\subsubsection{How it works}

\subsubsection{Example}

Insurers will not trust AXI's database - we are an interested party. They will trust a public log they can read and audit independently.

% ----------------------------- 3.2 High-Risk Command Vault
\newpage
\subsection{High-Risk Command Vault}

\subsubsection{The problem}
Fleet-wide actions are dangerous. "Lock 47 bikes" is one click, and it has to be reversible, auditable, and need more than one person to run. Most operator dashboards have a single-password override - that is how fleets get held ransom.

\subsubsection{The solution}
A multi-person approval gate. AXI staff propose a high-risk command (lock the fleet, override every route, send a mass broadcast, withdraw funds). A pre-set group of approvers must sign. Once enough approve (e.g. 2 of 3), anyone can run the command - including a regulator if AXI staff disappear.

\subsubsection{States the command goes through}

\subsubsection{Example}

\begin{scenariobox}[title=Mary's bike battery overheats]
KMG~117P shows battery temperature 52$^\circ$C - fire risk. Brian proposes "lock the 3 bikes in this depot" (needs 2 of 3 approvals).

\vspace{2pt}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, nosep]
  \item Joy approves \enspace$\cdot$\enspace 1 of 2 in
  \item ROAM ops manager approves \enspace$\cdot$\enspace 2 of 2 \enspace$\rightarrow$\enspace ready to run
  \item Anyone runs the command \enspace$\rightarrow$\enspace bikes locked
\end{itemize}

The chain shows who proposed, who approved, when, and what.
\end{scenariobox}

\subsubsection{Why on-chain, not Slack approvals}
A Slack approval can be denied later. An on-chain approval cannot. The audit trail is the product - insurers and regulators want proof of good governance before they price premiums or grant licences.

\subsubsection{Roles}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{3.8cm} p{11.5cm}}
\toprule
{\bfseries\color{axiprimary}Contract} & \texttt{HighRiskCommandVault.sol} (\textbf{not upgradeable}) \\
\midrule
Approval threshold & 1 to 10 approvers (set per command) \\
Expiry window & 1 minute to 30 days \\
Replay safety & Each command has a unique ID, so the same command cannot run twice \\
Payload check & The proposal locks the exact payload; running it must use the same bytes \\
Anyone can run it & Once approved, any address can run it - no AXI lock-in \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{notebox}[title=Why not upgradeable]
An upgradeable approval gate is a back door. The Vault is permanent - operators see the exact rules they are approving, and AXI cannot silently change them later.
\end{notebox}

% ----------------------------- 3.3 Credential Revocation Registry
\newpage
\subsection{Credential Revocation Registry}

\subsubsection{The problem}
Driver licences, NTSA inspection certificates, and insurance binders expire or get revoked. Today fleets find out at the roadside check (around \$300 fine + tow-truck bill). Insurers find out at claim time (around \$5{,}000 loss). Verification is still paper-based.

\subsubsection{The solution}
Each credential gets a unique fingerprint stored on-chain. When the issuer (NTSA, insurance, fleet manager) cancels it, they post the cancellation. Anyone - insurer, regulator, the rider app - can instantly check whether a credential is still valid. Only the original issuer can cancel their own credential.

\subsubsection{How it works}

\begin{center}
\adjustbox{max width=0.8\textwidth, max totalheight=5cm, center}{
\begin{tikzpicture}[node distance=2.6cm and 1.4cm]
  \node[seqactor] (op) {AXI staff};
  \node[seqactor, right=of op] (axi) {AXI\\backend};
  \node[seqactor, right=of axi] (reg) {Registry};
  \node[seqactor, right=of reg] (verifier) {Anyone};

  \draw[seqarrow] (op) -- node[above, font=\scriptsize] {cancel credential} (axi);
  \draw[seqarrow] (axi) -- node[above, font=\scriptsize] {post cancel + reason} (reg);
  \draw[seqarrow] (reg) to[bend right=20] node[below, font=\scriptsize] {confirmation} (axi);
  \draw[seqarrow] (verifier) to[bend right=25] node[above, font=\scriptsize] {is it cancelled?} (reg);
  \draw[seqarrow] (reg) to[bend right=35] node[below, font=\scriptsize] {yes} (verifier);
\end{tikzpicture}
}
\end{center}

\subsubsection{Example}

\begin{scenariobox}[title=ROAM rider Wanjiku's NTSA inspection]
Inspection expires on 2026-05-15. The NTSA officer cancels it on-chain (reason: expired).

\vspace{2pt}
At the next checkpoint, the inspector scans the QR code on Wanjiku's bike. The app checks the registry $\rightarrow$ cancelled. Wanjiku gets a polite warning, not a fraud charge. AXI avoids a court date.
\end{scenariobox}

\subsubsection{Why on-chain, not the NTSA portal}
NTSA's portal goes down often. The chain does not. And insurers outside Kenya can verify without trusting any single Kenyan database.

\subsubsection{Roles}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{3.8cm} p{11.5cm}}
\toprule
{\bfseries\color{axiprimary}Contract} & \texttt{CredentialRevocationRegistry.sol} (upgradeable) \\
\midrule
\texttt{ISSUER\_ROLE} & Only the original issuer can cancel a credential they issued \\
\texttt{revoke()} & Cannot cancel the same credential twice (preserves the first reason) \\
\texttt{revokeBatch()} & Cancel up to 100 at once (useful when rotating an issuer's key) \\
\texttt{unrevoke()} & Admin-only emergency undo (used if an issuer key is compromised) \\
\texttt{pause()} & Emergency stop \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Three flows, every day}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Daily summary at midnight UTC} --- AXI builds one fingerprint of the day's events and posts it to peaq (and to Polygon as backup). Anyone can pick a random event from yesterday and prove it was in that summary.
  \item \textbf{Per-event signing (live)} --- when an AI agent flags a serious event, the AXI backend signs it with a hardware-backed key and posts the proof to peaq within seconds. The case UI shows a "verified" badge.
  \item \textbf{Public verification (anytime)} --- \texttt{verify.aximobility.com/api/verify/[hash]} returns proof anyone can check against peaq directly. No trust in AXI needed.
\end{itemize}
